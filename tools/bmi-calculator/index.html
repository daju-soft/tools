What is BMI?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a simple calculation that uses your height and weight to estimate whether you are in a healthy weight range. It was developed by Belgian mathematician Adolphe Quetelet in the 1830s and is now used worldwide as a quick screening tool by doctors and health organisations.

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in metres. For example someone who is 1.75 metres tall and weighs 70 kilograms has a BMI of 70 divided by 1.75 squared which equals 22.9. This falls in the normal weight range of 18.5 to 24.9.

BMI Categories

A BMI below 18.5 is considered underweight. Between 18.5 and 24.9 is the normal or healthy weight range. Between 25 and 29.9 is considered overweight. A BMI of 30 or above is considered obese. These categories apply to adults aged 18 and over. Different scales are used for children and teenagers.

Limitations of BMI

BMI is a useful screening tool but it has significant limitations. It does not measure body fat directly and cannot distinguish between muscle and fat. A heavily muscled athlete may have a high BMI despite having very low body fat. An older person may have a normal BMI but a high percentage of body fat. BMI also does not account for where fat is distributed on the body which affects health risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is BMI accurate?

BMI is a useful population-level screening tool but it is not a perfect measure of individual health. It works best as a starting point for conversations with healthcare professionals rather than a definitive health assessment. For a complete picture of your health, consult a doctor who can consider additional factors.

Is my data stored?

No. All calculations happen in your browser. No data is sent to any server.
