How to Calculate a Tip

To calculate a tip, multiply the bill amount by the tip percentage divided by 100. For a $50 bill with a 20 percent tip, the calculation is 50 times 0.20 which equals $10. The total is $50 plus $10 which equals $60. To split between people, divide the total by the number of people.

Standard Tip Amounts

In the United States, 15 to 20 percent is the standard tip for restaurant service. 10 percent is considered below average for good service. 20 to 25 percent is generous and appropriate for excellent service. For other services like hair salons and taxi rides, 15 to 20 percent is typical. In many other countries tipping is less common or the amounts differ significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax amount?

Either is acceptable. Most people tip on the pre-tax subtotal which is slightly less. Some tip on the full total including tax. The difference on a typical restaurant bill is small either way.

Is my data stored?

No. All calculations happen instantly in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server.
