What is a QR Code?

A QR code — Quick Response code — is a two-dimensional barcode that can store text, URLs, contact information, and more. Smartphones can scan QR codes with their camera to instantly open a website, save a contact, connect to WiFi, or perform other actions without typing anything.

QR codes were invented in 1994 by a Japanese company called Denso Wave for tracking car parts. They became mainstream when smartphones made scanning easy. Today they are used everywhere — restaurant menus, payment systems, event tickets, product packaging, and marketing materials.

QR Code Types

URL QR codes open a website when scanned. Text QR codes display plain text. Email QR codes open a compose window with the address pre-filled. Phone QR codes dial a number. WiFi QR codes connect automatically to a network without typing the password. Each type uses a specific format that smartphones recognise.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are QR codes free to use?

Yes. The QR code standard is open and free to use. You can generate and use QR codes for any purpose without paying any fees or royalties.

Do QR codes expire?

QR codes generated by this tool do not expire. The QR code simply encodes the text or URL you provide. As long as that URL or information is still valid the QR code will work. Some third-party QR code services use redirect URLs that can expire — this tool does not use redirects.

What is the maximum amount of data a QR code can store?

A QR code can store up to 4,296 alphanumeric characters. However shorter content produces a simpler QR code that is easier to scan. Keep URLs short and use a URL shortener if needed for the most scannable QR codes.

Is my data safe?

Yes. QR codes are generated entirely in your browser. Your content is never sent to any server.
